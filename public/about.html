A little bit about us...

The Red Dunes Camp was formed in the 1980s by a group of Bedouins who wanted to share their culture and way of life with visitors. The camp is made up of traditional Bedouin tents, and it offers a variety of activities, such as camel riding, hiking, and stargazing.

The camp is located in a remote part of the desert, and it is only accessible by 4x4 vehicles. This makes it a great place to escape the hustle and bustle of everyday life and experience the peace and tranquility of the desert. The camp is also a great place to stargaze, as the skies are dark and clear.

If you are looking for a unique and unforgettable experience, then a stay at a camp in Wadi Rum is a great option. You will get to experience the beauty of the desert, learn about Bedouin culture, and make memories that will last a lifetime.

FAQs

The best time to visit Wadi Rum is during the spring (April to May) or autumn (September to October). The weather is mild during these months, and there is less chance of rain. The summer months (June to August) can be very hot, and the winter months (December to February) can be cold and rainy.

There are many activities available in Wadi Rum, including:
  • 4x4 desert safaris
  • Hiking
  • Camel riding
  • Rock climbing
  • Stargazing

Here are some of the things you should bring on your trip to Wadi Rum:
  • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  • Comfortable shoes
  • Long-sleeved shirt and pants
  • Water bottle
  • Flashlight
